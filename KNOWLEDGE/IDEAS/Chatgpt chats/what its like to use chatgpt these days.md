### The part that breaks the intuition: **hysteresis**

Rubber doesn’t just squish and spring back. It **eats energy**.

When rubber deforms:

- some energy comes back
    
- some turns into heat (internal friction)
    

That loss is called **hysteresis**. It’s why:

- solid tires feel slow
    
- they get hot
    
- they transmit vibration instead of filtering it
    

Air, by contrast, is almost lossless at these timescales:

- compress → expand
    
- very little energy lost
    
- very fast response
    

So an air tire is not “a balloon.”  
It’s a **spring with near-perfect rebound**.

A solid rubber tire is a **shock absorber that never stops absorbing**.

---

On a bike:

- big wheels
    
- flexible frame
    
- human power compensates losses
    

On your scooter:

- small wheels
    
- rigid frame
    
- electric motor tuned for efficiency
    
- electronics bolted to the frame
    

So when you run solid tires:

- every micro-bump becomes heat + vibration
    
- motor draws more current
    
- controller works harder
    
- range drops
    
- components loosen and fatigue
    

The tire _feels_ tougher, but the system is under more stress.
